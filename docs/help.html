How to use this site

Everything you need to find, select, and copy community organisation contacts for workshop outreach.

🚀 Quick start

  1. 1
    Choose a state or territory

    From the homepage, click the card for the region you want to search. Each card shows how many community contacts are in the database.

  2. 2
    Enter a location and radius

    Type a postcode (e.g. 5000) or suburb name (e.g. Adelaide) into the search box. Choose your radius — 10–20 km works well in metro areas, 50–100 km for regional and remote coverage.

  3. 3
    Copy what you need

    Use Copy All Emails to grab every email in the results, or tick individual organisations first to copy just a targeted selection.

🛠 Feature guide

  • Category filter Narrow results by organisation type — for example, Allied Health, Early Childhood & Family Services, Disability Advocacy, Parent/Carer Support, or any of the 15 category types — using the dropdown next to the search box.
  • Email only Toggle this checkbox to hide organisations that don't have a public email address, so you only see contacts you can email directly.
  • Checkboxes Tick individual rows to build a custom selection. Use the checkbox in the column header to select or deselect everything at once.
  • Copy All Emails When no rows are checked, copies every email address in the current results to your clipboard — one address per line, ready to paste into your email client's BCC field.
  • Copy N Selected When rows are checked, the copy button changes to show how many are selected. Copying in this mode uses the richer Organisation Name <email@address> format, which most email clients will display as a named recipient.
  • Website / Source links Where available, links to the organisation's website or the source where the contact was found are shown in the Links column. These open in a new tab.
Tip: Selecting rows and then toggling Email only will reset your selection — so apply any filters before you start ticking.

💬 Frequently asked questions

  • What kind of organisations are included?

    The database covers 15 categories of community organisations relevant to autism workshop promotion: Allied Health providers, Disability Advocacy groups, Early Childhood & Family Services, NDIS providers, Parent/Carer Support groups, Cultural & Multicultural Organisations, Councils & Libraries, Sport & Recreation programs, and more. The full list appears in the category dropdown on each state page.

  • Why do some organisations show no email address?

    Not every organisation publishes a direct email on their website. Some use web-based contact forms instead. Where possible we've included a link to the organisation's website so you can find their preferred contact method.

  • Where does the data come from?

    Contacts are sourced from publicly available directories, government listings, peak body member lists, and organisation websites. Only publicly listed contact details are included — no private or scraped data is used.

  • How current is the data?

    The database was compiled in early 2026. Organisation details can change — if you find an out-of-date contact, the organisation's website will always have the most current information.

  • Why are some regions or categories sparse?

    Coverage is strongest in metropolitan areas and for well-established organisation types. Remote and very regional areas may have fewer results. Try increasing your search radius to capture nearby towns and regional centres.

  • What's the best way to email a curated list of organisations?

    Tick the organisations you want, then use Copy N Selected. This copies addresses in Name <email> format. Paste the whole block into your email client's BCC field — most clients (Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail) will recognise each line as a separate named recipient automatically.

  • Can I search across multiple states at once?

    Not currently — each state has its own page and database. For cross-border searches (e.g. the ACT/NSW border region), run a search on each relevant state page and combine your results.

  • How is this different from the School Contacts tool?

    The School Contacts tool focuses on schools (government, Catholic, independent). This tool covers the broader community sector — health services, support groups, councils, libraries, recreation programs, and other organisations that can help promote autism workshops beyond the school network.
