# Sourcing Plan by Category (NSW-first)

This plan prioritizes publicly available, high-trust directories and organisation websites.

## General sourcing rules

- Use official or established directory sources first.
- Capture only publicly listed organisational contacts.
- Do not scrape staff personal contact details.
- Prefer generic inboxes (for example `info@`, `contact@`) over personal addresses.
- Record source URLs and a verification date for each contact.

## Category playbook

1. Schools & Education Networks (non-school orgs)
- Start sources: regional education networks, P&C associations, principal/professional associations, homeschool/alternative education networks.
- Why it matters: high leverage channels for workshop referrals.

2. Early Childhood & Family Services
- Start sources: family support NGOs, early intervention providers, parenting support programs.
- Why it matters: reaches parents early in support journey.

3. Disability & NDIS Services
- Start sources: NDIS provider directories, disability peak bodies, local disability support organisations.
- Why it matters: direct alignment with autism support ecosystem.

4. Allied Health (OT, Speech, Psych)
- Start sources: clinic directories, provider websites, health network listings.
- Why it matters: clinicians can refer families to workshops.

5. Parent/Carer Support Organisations
- Start sources: carer organisations, parent-led community groups, peer support groups.
- Why it matters: trusted peer-to-peer promotion pathway.

6. Community Centres & Neighbourhood Houses
- Start sources: neighbourhood centre directories, local centre websites.
- Why it matters: noticeboards, newsletters, and local event calendars.

7. Councils & Libraries
- Start sources: local council community pages, library event networks.
- Why it matters: broad community reach and trusted communication channels.

8. Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander Services
- Start sources: Aboriginal community-controlled organisations, local Aboriginal services.
- Why it matters: culturally safer outreach and inclusive engagement.

9. Multicultural & Settlement Services
- Start sources: settlement providers, multicultural associations, language community groups.
- Why it matters: supports culturally and linguistically diverse families.

10. Faith-Based Community Organisations
- Start sources: church welfare services, faith community support programs.
- Why it matters: active local networks with strong family connections.

11. Youth Services
- Start sources: youth centres, youth NGOs, local youth wellbeing programs.
- Why it matters: engages older students and carers through youth channels.

12. Health Services & Primary Health Networks
- Start sources: PHN directories, community health services, family health services.
- Why it matters: referral pathways from frontline health services.

13. NGOs/Charities (family, disability, mental health)
- Start sources: charity directories, national NGO local branches.
- Why it matters: established outreach and program communication channels.

14. Sport, Recreation & Inclusion Programs
- Start sources: inclusive sports programs, disability recreation groups, councils’ recreation listings.
- Why it matters: practical community engagement touchpoints.

15. Local Media & Community Promotion Channels
- Start sources: community radio, local newspapers, community event newsletters/calendars.
- Why it matters: broadcast amplification for workshop promotions.

## Sourcing sequence (recommended)

1. Build NSW seed list for categories 2, 3, 5, 6, and 7 first (highest likely promotion ROI).
2. Expand to categories 8 and 9 to improve inclusive reach.
3. Add remaining categories to increase breadth.
4. Roll process state-by-state after NSW workflow is stable.
